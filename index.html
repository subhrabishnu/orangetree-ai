Program Modules
What participants will learn across the program
Curriculum

Six Structured Modules — From Concepts to Hands-On Practice

Each module builds on the last, taking participants from zero understanding of AI to confidently using AI tools in their day-to-day work.

01

What is AI? The Big Picture

Foundation
  • AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning
  • A brief history of AI and where we are today
  • How AI differs from traditional software
  • Real-world AI examples you already use daily
  • The AI hype vs. reality: what it can and can't do
02

AI Across Industries

Context
  • AI in healthcare, finance, retail, and education
  • How companies like Google, Amazon, startups use AI
  • AI use cases relevant to your sector
  • Job roles that work with AI today
  • Career opportunities the AI wave is creating
03

AI Tools & Platforms

Practical
  • Tour: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot
  • AI tools for writing, design, analysis, and code
  • No-code AI platforms and how to use them
  • Choosing the right tool for the right task
  • Free vs. paid tools: what's worth it
04

Data Basics & AI Readiness

Data
  • Why data is the fuel of AI
  • Structured vs. unstructured vs. labelled data
  • What makes good data vs. bad data
  • Assessing your organisation's data readiness
  • Introduction to data quality and cleaning
05

Ethical AI & Responsible Use

Ethics
  • AI bias: what it is and why it matters
  • Privacy, consent, and data security with AI
  • Deepfakes, misinformation, and misuse
  • Regulatory landscape: EU AI Act, India's framework
  • How to use AI responsibly in your role
06

Hands-On: AI in Action

Workshop
  • Live tool walkthroughs with real tasks
  • Prompt writing basics: getting better outputs
  • Group activity: solving a real problem with AI
  • Building a personal AI toolkit for your work
  • Q&A and personalised guidance session

Who Is This For
Designed for two distinct audiences — same program, fully relevant to both
Target Audience

You Don't Need a Tech Background. Just Curiosity.

AI Fundamentals is built for people at the start of their AI journey — whether you're entering the workforce or just a few years in.

💼

Working Professionals · 0–5 Years

You're in the early stages of your career and starting to see AI mentioned everywhere — in tools, job descriptions, and conversations. This program gives you the foundation to understand, use, and talk about AI confidently at work.

  • You work in marketing, finance, operations, HR, or sales
  • Your job is changing because of AI and you want to keep up
  • You want to add AI skills to your resume
  • You've tried ChatGPT but want to use it properly
  • You want to understand AI without learning to code
🎓

College Students

You're about to enter a job market where AI fluency is already an expectation, not a bonus. This program helps you understand what AI is, how it works in real companies, and how to use it to stand out — before you even graduate.

  • You're in any stream — arts, commerce, science, or engineering
  • You want to future-proof your career before campus placements
  • You've heard about AI but never had structured learning
  • You want a certificate that signals AI literacy to employers
  • You're curious about AI-related career paths

Learning Outcomes
What participants will be able to do after completing the program
After This Program

Concrete Skills, Not Just Concepts

By the end of AI Fundamentals, every participant leaves with practical skills they can apply the very next day.

01

Explain AI Clearly to Anyone

Confidently describe what AI, ML, and LLMs are — to colleagues, managers, or interviewers — without technical jargon.

02

Use AI Tools Effectively at Work

Write better prompts, choose the right AI tool, and use AI to draft, summarise, analyse, and automate tasks in their current role.

03

Spot AI Opportunities in Their Domain

Identify where AI can save time, reduce errors, or improve outputs in their specific job function or industry.

04

Navigate AI Responsibly

Understand the ethical risks of AI — bias, privacy, misinformation — and know what responsible use looks like in practice.

05

Ask Better Questions About Data

Understand why data quality matters for AI and have meaningful conversations about data readiness within their team.

06

Prepare for What's Next

Know which AI topics to explore next — generative AI, data analytics, or sector-specific AI — with a clear path forward.
