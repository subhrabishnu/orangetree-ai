01 · Program Modules

Six Structured Modules — From Concepts to Hands-On Practice

Each module builds on the last, taking participants from zero understanding of AI to confidently using AI tools in their day-to-day work.

MODULE 01
Foundation

What is AI? The Big Picture

  • AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning
  • A brief history of AI and where we are today
  • How AI is different from traditional software
  • Real-world AI examples you already use daily
  • The AI hype vs. reality: what it can and can't do
MODULE 02
Context

AI Across Industries

  • AI in healthcare, finance, retail, and education
  • How companies like Google, Amazon, and startups use AI
  • AI use cases relevant to your sector
  • Job roles that work with AI today
  • Career opportunities the AI wave is creating
MODULE 03
Practical

AI Tools & Platforms

  • Tour of major AI tools: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot
  • AI tools for writing, design, analysis, and code
  • No-code AI platforms and how to use them
  • Choosing the right tool for the right task
  • Free vs. paid tools: what's worth it
MODULE 04
Data

Data Basics & AI Readiness

  • Why data is the fuel of AI
  • Types of data: structured, unstructured, and labelled
  • What makes good data vs. bad data
  • How to assess your organisation's data readiness
  • Introduction to data quality and cleaning concepts
MODULE 05
Ethics

Ethical AI & Responsible Use

  • AI bias: what it is and why it matters
  • Privacy, consent, and data security with AI
  • Deepfakes, misinformation, and misuse of AI
  • Regulatory landscape (EU AI Act, India's AI framework)
  • How to use AI responsibly in your role
MODULE 06
Workshop

Hands-On: AI in Action

  • Live tool walkthroughs with real tasks
  • Prompt writing basics: getting better AI outputs
  • Group activity: solving a real problem with AI
  • Building a personal AI toolkit for your work
  • Q&A and personalised guidance session

03 · Learning Outcomes

Concrete Skills, Not Just Concepts

By the end of AI Fundamentals, every participant leaves with practical skills they can apply in their role or studies the very next day.

01

Explain AI Clearly to Anyone

Confidently describe what AI, ML, and LLMs are — to colleagues, managers, or interviewers — without technical jargon.

02

Use AI Tools Effectively at Work

Write better prompts, choose the right AI tool, and use AI to draft, summarise, analyse, and automate tasks in their current role.

03

Spot AI Opportunities in Their Domain

Identify where AI can save time, reduce errors, or improve outputs in their specific job function or industry.

04

Navigate AI Responsibly

Understand the ethical risks of AI — bias, privacy, misinformation — and know what responsible AI use looks like in practice.

05

Ask Better Questions About Data

Understand why data quality matters for AI, and have meaningful conversations about data readiness within their team or organisation.

06

Prepare for What's Next

Know which AI topics to explore next — generative AI, data analytics, or sector-specific AI — and have a clear path forward.
